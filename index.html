For 40 years, the concentration of my law practice has been the representation of homeowners associations (including condominiums) and property owners associations, both as counsel to associations and as their trial lawyer in litigation. The body of law that embraces the development, establishment, and operation of residential owners associations is called Community Association Law. In addition to interpretation of and litigation concerning condominium, unit ownership, and planned community statutes in several states, my representation of community associations also has included corporate, contract, real property, real covenants, land use and zoning, construction, employment, and civil rights law.

While I have been certified as a Superior Court Mediator in North Carolina for the last five years as I continued my legal practice, I will now dedicate my time to serving as a mediator. Since I have been an arbitrator for construction disputes and represented clients in many arbitrations, both construction and commercial, I am also at the disposition of parties in a dispute to be their arbitrator and make binding decisions to resolve disputes.
